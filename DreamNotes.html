Dream Notes

UI/UX Design & Development

Background.

Dream notes is a small lightweight task manager and notes app that I designed for myself.

I've been using a similar app for some time now on my phone and I wanted to design something with a more fluid and user-friendly UI that stimulated me to be more productive.


The Problem

The problem is that all of the current local task management apps for android look clunky and out of date, except the google and Microsoft ones, which I'm not going to mention because Google and Microsoft are market giants and they pack more than what is needed for a simple management app.


The Solution and Goals

Researching most of the competitor apps I figured out that most of them are lacking visual design which makes me want to not use them. The goal was to create a clean and beautiful app that is easy to use on a day-to-day basis which beats the competition UI Design-wise.

Preview

Competition Research

In order for me to understand what makes Dream Notes a worthwhile application to use I downloaded a few other task managers and went through them thoroughly, seeing what I had to do better and analyzing what they all had in common.

01. To Do List

To Do List is one of the top competitors on the market with more than 10 million downloads and a user rating of 4.9. It's the first app I did research on and honestly I was mind boggled how it had such high statistics.

The overall Design Language is really poor. The application looks like something that is made with one of these Make it Yourself app-editors that are around the web. The user experience is simple and straight forward which I respect, but at the same time, I'd make more effort.

Right off the bat, I saw that with little to no effort I can make a better job on the Design Language and User Experience.

02. Tasks.org

Tasks.org is the second task manager I reviewed with fewer downloads capping at 100 000 and about the same user rating.

Simmilar to To Do List, they both have the same home page layout, although tasks.org seems to have improved the design language a bit.

I don't approve all of the items on the side menu, I don't like how it looks I don't like how it feels and it throws me away from using the application because it looks too complicated and as if there's too much going on for something as simple as a local task manager.

After going through this one I was still set on doing something with a better visual representation.

03. Tasks

Tasks is the third application I decided to download, with more than 100 000 downloads it seemed like a descent application.

Afetr using this for 5 minutes I started questioning why this exists at all.

Everything was horrible I'm not going to bother saying anything more.

Research Outcome

After taking a day to research and use the following 3 apps I figured out that they were pretty simple and easy to use, they did the job well even though visually it felt really bad using them, except the third one which was overall a bad application to use, both visually and functionally.

I came to a conclusion that at the end of the day, it's ok if the end product has fewer functions and is more clean and snappy. Using more complex task managers made me feel overwhelmed, I really didn't like having too much going on for something as simple as writing stuff down.

The Product

I started off by making some really simple wireframes, although I already had the vision I was going for in my mind, so It kind of felt unnecessary at the time being, also seeing that I was doing this project for myself.

I then mapped a super simple user flow, which for the size of the project and functionallity wasn't hard to figure.

I mostly pondered on where to place the invitation hyperlink which went into the settings page.

Yes, I skipped the authentication for accounts because I wanted the use experience to be fast.

After that I moved to the Hi-Fidelity design, I skipped the low fidelity one because I decided I would poke at the elements one by one untill I had them look propper.

Existing User Research

I gave Thomas a link to my prototype so he could check out the slides and give me some feedback on the project.

Q: What are your thoughts about the product and do you see yourself using it daily?

Georgi: It looks pretty clean, I definitely would use this on my phone. I tried using ClickUp but that had too many features so I stuck with my Samsung notes.

Q: What do you think should be changed or implemented based on the current prototype ?

Georgi: Maybe you should add a feature where the fonts can be changed to bigger sizes, sometimes I use my phone without glasses and I have a hard time seeing some app texts.

I added a feature to the settings page as per Georgi's request so the text could scale 10%.

Product Showcase

Even though some of the people we talked to liked having a more flashy design, we still decided to keep the minimal mid-fidelity approach because of the ease of use. In the long term, we thought that the larger companies would rather choose something which is to the point, rather than go for colors and illustrations.

Active Note Task Pages

The active tasks and notes pages aren't really much different from the reviewed apps. The only difference is the layout and styling. I re-thought the layout and made a clean typographical hierarchy that catches the user's eye at a glance. This clean approach is already proven to work better in most case scenarios, for example, Apple uses similar design approaches for their apps and it has proven to work flawlessly, people love it, including me.

One trouble point was the Title/Name field placement and sizing, because of the larger text size it would tend to overflow or stretch to the second row, which I didn't want to happen. It took me some time and I figure out that I would make the text into a slider in larger names, where it would constantly slide from the right to the left.

The title fields are the only thing in the entire project that I dislike but couldn't find a better solution or fix to without changing the entire typography sizing throughout the app. And I would change if I could figure out a better approach.

Menu

The menu is super simple and easy to navigate.

I really love how the menu works and how easy it is to navigate and make tasks with it. It's something that the competitors are lacking so its a key feature.

I presume that the middle circle for adding tasks and notes would be hard on the developers for integration.

Modules



Colors

The colors choices are simple and generally used all around the web and on mobile, which makes them easy to adapt to and rove to be good for orientation and overall good for a snappy user experience.


Typography

Yantramanav is a new typeface that I recently stumbled on and liked a lot. It's clean simple bold and to the point. This is the main font I chose for the project and I think it looks good o the final results.

I used roboto for the menu fonts, after testing different fonts I saw that it looked best on the menu compared to Yantramanav.



Final Thoughts

This was an amazing project to work on, it was also really fun working with the Ulties development team [ www.ulties.com ] Sadly we never finished the product because the developers dropped the project mid-way after. They decided to scratch the whole thing after version 2 of the beta testing. Even though it didn't work out I still decided to showcase the project because I put in too many hours for it to go to waste.

If we did eventually make it past the beta testing, the design would have had probably even more changes to it.
