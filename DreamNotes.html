Dream Notes

UI/UX Design & Development

Background.

Dream notes is a small lightweight task manager and notes app that I designed for myself.

I've been using a similar app for some time now on my phone and I wanted to design something with a more fluid and user-friendly UI that stimulated me to be more productive.


The Problem

The problem is that all of the current local task management apps for android look clunky and out of date, except the google and Microsoft ones, which I'm not going to mention because Google and Microsoft are market giants and they pack more than what is needed for a simple management app.


The Solution and Goals

Researching most of the competitor apps I figured out that most of them are lacking visual design which makes me want to not use them. The goal was to create a clean and beautiful app that is easy to use on a day-to-day basis which beats the competition UI Design-wise.

Preview

Competition Research

In order for me to understand what makes Dream Notes a worthwhile application to use I downloaded a few other task managers and went through them thoroughly, seeing what I had to do better and analyzing what they all had in common.

01. To Do List

To Do List is one of the top competitors on the market with more than 10 million downloads and a user rating of 4.9. It's the first app I did research on and honestly I was mind boggled how it had such high statistics.

The overall Design Language is really poor. The application looks like something that is made with one of these Make it Yourself app-editors that are around the web. The user experience is simple and straight forward which I respect, but at the same time, I'd make more effort.

Right off the bat, I saw that with little to no effort I can make a better job on the Design Language and User Experience.

02. Tasks.org

Tasks.org is the second task manager I reviewed with fewer downloads capping at 100 000 and about the same user rating.

Simmilar to To Do List, they both have the same home page layout, although tasks.org seems to have improved the design language a bit.

I don't approve all of the items on the side menu, I don't like how it looks I don't like how it feels and it throws me away from using the application because it looks too complicated and as if there's too much going on for something as simple as a local task manager.

After going through this one I was still set on doing something with a better visual representation.

03. Tasks

Tasks is the third application I decided to download, with more than 100 000 downloads it seemed like a descent application.

Afetr using this for 5 minutes I started questioning why this exists at all.

Everything was horrible I'm not going to bother saying anything more.

The Product

Even though some of the people we talked to liked having a more flashy design, we still decided to keep the minimal mid-fidelity approach because of the ease of use. In the long term, we thought that the larger companies would rather choose something which is to the point, rather than go for colors and illustrations.

Other Elements


Modules

Because Uptime has more than 30 pages, the whole Project is based on modules, this way its more easier to add more featurets without having to re work entire pages. There are only two fixed elements which are the side menu and the upper search/account bar.

This makes it super easy both for me to design new content on demand, and also for the developers to create a pre made template.


Menu

The menu is super simple and easy to navigate.

I really love how simple and clean the menu turned out. We also though about making a variable menu, where the navigation links would change if there was a demand for more functionality.


Colors

The colors of the project consist of carefully selected colors, designed to stand out across the competitive set. They aim to provide a soft feel that doesn’t create excess focus points that obstruct attention.

We also intended to have a Dark Theme, so the colors were selected with that in mind for future revamps that included that.


Typography

I deicded to use a market standartized font that is well known by a variety of users. Nunito is a well balanced Sans Serif with rounded terminals. Nunito has been designed mainly to be used as a display font but is useable as a text font too. Nunito has been designed to be used freely across the internet by web browsers on desktop computers, laptops and mobile devices.

To be honest I didn't really spend so much time into chosing the right font for this project, I was more focused on pushing things so we can move to a demo version release as fast as possible.



Branding

I didn't really have much time to do a proper logo or brand development because the project wasn't ready to be launched. So We came up with this small logo. It started off as a doodle and we decided to just go with it until we had the time to do proper brand development.



Final Thoughts

This was an amazing project to work on, it was also really fun working with the Ulties development team [ www.ulties.com ] Sadly we never finished the product because the developers dropped the project mid-way after. They decided to scratch the whole thing after version 2 of the beta testing. Even though it didn't work out I still decided to showcase the project because I put in too many hours for it to go to waste.

If we did eventually make it past the beta testing, the design would have had probably even more changes to it.
