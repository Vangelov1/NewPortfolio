Uptime Monitor

UI/UX Design & Development

Background.

Uptime monitor is a project that I was tasked to work on by Ulties, which is a Danish web design and development agency. I was previously working with the company on several small-scale projects for their clients. Then they asked me to join in as a UX and UI developer on Uptime. At the time I was also working on other smaller projects on freelance which I dropped to make up the time. The company wanted to make a website monitoring service, their goal was to make it with more features and a better user experience than their competitors at a more affordable price. I was really happy to accept their offer, as the project is something I really wanted to showcase on my portfolio.


The Problem

We had to make a stunning user experience for something that had over 100 features and make it in such a way that both small clients like self-employed people could use it for their small business website and also have it scale for larger companies and corporations.


The Solution and Goals

When I started my research I figured out that most of our competitors had a traditional full-height website layout which was pretty badly organized. For such a scale of function and management, one would need to have a dashboard-style design. So we decided to make our project with a Dashboard layout, rather than the ordinary full height scroll website. Back when I made the initial design, there was only one more competitor that had done that, currently It seems that there are more services that have incorporated this approach. There seem to be some new ones out too.

We also decided to copycat apple's IOS design and make everything in its own separate module, we thought it would be best if we kept the Design Language to a minimum, having it nearly mid-fidelity. We wanted everything to be super clean without any extra colors, illustrations or fancy animations, other than some smooth transitions.

Prototype Preview

User Research

In order to understand the task at hand and the user needs, I ended up talking to people, asking them what they thought about our approach giving them some initial prototypes for comparison. I contacted people in several development discord channels because I had to talk to people with actual knowledge and experience in using such software or at least understanding the basic function of it.

And here is what they said.

Q: What are your thoughts about using our SASS app and would you prefer a full-length layout or a 100vh dashboard-style one?

Q: What do you think of our prototype and it's design and structure.

Conclusions

Around 85% of the people we talked to seemed to like the approach we were going with, some of them of course had thing's that they didn't like, for example, the lack of colors, but all in all they were minor things that didn't impact the functionality of the design and how things were structured. We took some small advice about alignments and some advice about the general service and made small adjustments. For example one of them was that a developer wanted to have dark mode, so its easy on the eyes. We decided to push that in version 2.

The Product

Even though some of the people we talked to liked having a more flashy design, we still decided to keep the minimal mid-fidelity approach because of the ease of use. In the long term, we thought that the larger companies would rather choose something which is to the point, rather than go for colors and illustrations.

Other Elements


Modules

Because Uptime has more than 30 pages, the whole Project is based on modules, this way its more easier to add more featurets without having to re work entire pages. There are only two fixed elements which are the side menu and the upper search/account bar.

This makes it super easy both for me to design new content on demand, and also for the developers to create a pre made template.


Menu

The menu is super simple and easy to navigate.

The menu layout and User Experience was the most fun thing for me to create. I really love how it turned out and I mostly love that this feature by itself beats most of the competition in ease of use.


Colors

The colors of the project consist of carefully selected colors, designed to stand out across the competitive set. They aim to provide a soft feel that doesn’t create excess focus points that obstruct attention.

We also intended to have a Dark Theme, so the colors were selected with that in mind for future revamps that included that.


Typography

I deicded to use a market standartized font that is well known by a variety of users. Nunito is a well balanced Sans Serif with rounded terminals. Nunito has been designed mainly to be used as a display font but is useable as a text font too. Nunito has been designed to be used freely across the internet by web browsers on desktop computers, laptops and mobile devices.

To be honest I didn't really spend so much time into chosing the right font for this project, I was more focused on pushing things so we can move to a demo version release as fast as possible.



Branding

One of the areas that I cut down on was branding. Essentially, branding would be one of the priority things that I would focus on but specifically for this project I decided to go simple and cut down on the design time for the logo and typography. The project itself is orientated towards functionality rather than aesthetics. With that in mind, I made a simple and clean logo, followed by a clean and bold typography that doesn’t go too hard on the eyes.



Final Thoughts

The end result within our minds is a perfect blend of simplicity and functionality. We managed to create a tool that is simple in nature but provides a very powerful spectrum of resources that help any scale of user to manage the health of their online presence, from large corporations to everyday individuals. Uptime monitor was designed to be able to scale as much as it is needed to.
